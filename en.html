CORVUS X8

Unmanned Aerial Vehicle for Rapid Delivery of Emergency Kits in Urban Contexts

The Urban Challenge

Cardiorespiratory arrest is one of the leading causes of preventable mortality in urban environments. In densely populated cities like Lisbon, traffic congestion severely conditions the response time of ground-based medical emergency services. We know that each minute passed without access to an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) reduces the probability of survival by approximately 10%.

Our Solution

The CORVUS X8 operates autonomously under human supervision, ensuring the delivery of the medical kit long before ground emergency services arrive.

  • Winch Delivery: Uses the commercial RDS2 system (A2Z Drone Delivery) to deploy the kit safely to the ground.
  • Safe Operation: The UAV does not land. It maintains a stable hover at an altitude of 25 meters, keeping the rotors far away from people and urban obstacles.
  • Critical Performance: Validated for a one-way operational range of 3.7 km, reaching the location in just 4 min 45 s.

Technical Specifications

Configuration Coaxial X8 octocopter. Offers high fault tolerance (propulsion redundancy) and extreme precision in hover.
Dimensions & Weight Refined Maximum Takeoff Mass (MTOM) of 22.7 kg. Optimized CFRP chassis integration.
Emergency Payload 5.0 kg payload/winch subsystem. Accommodates a custom case with an AED and medical consumables (totaling up to 3.5 kg).
Propulsion 8x T-Motor U8 Lite KV100 motors, G28x9.2 CF propellers, and T-Motor Alpha 60A 12S ESCs.
Power & Endurance Redundant power supply from 2x Li-ion 12S4P packs (40 Ah total). Total mission duration of 18 min and 7 s.
Avionics & Control Cube Orange+ Flight Controller with a companion computer (RPi 4) and redundant C2 via a dual-operator LTE network.

Simulation and Navigation

Software In The Loop (SITL) simulation environment and Gazebo Harmonic for UAV dynamics validation (OctaQuad X) and obstacle detection in a hospital urban scenario.
